What is the issue?

Over 1.3 million people depend on buses every day to get to school, work, appointments, and other important places. Yet, bus service is often slow, delayed, and unreliable. In fact, 128 out of 243 (52%) of frequent bus lines are regularly delayed enough that riders wait 10 minutes or more, even though the scheduled wait time is usually five minutes. This problem is called “bus bunching,” and it can make riders wait 10 to 18 minutes for buses that are supposed to arrive every five minutes.

Traffic congestion and a lack of dedicated bus lanes make buses slower than other forms of transportation. This especially affects low-income communities, seniors, students, and essential workers who rely on buses as their main way to travel. When buses are inefficient, it creates inequality because not everyone has access to faster options

Bus efficiency is not just about speed, it is about fairness, access, and equal opportunity for everyone in the city.

About Us

Who We Are

We are NYC public high school students working to improve quality of life by raising awareness about bus equity and efficiency. Through this website, we provide resources, statistics, and solutions to how fair and reliable bus service can positively impact students, workers, seniors, and low-income communities who rely on public transportation.

Mission Statement

The mission of this website is to raise awareness about bus efficiency and equity. Many people depend on buses every day, and when buses are slow, unreliable, or overcrowded, it impacts school, work, and daily life. We aim to promote a bus system that is reliable, efficient, and accessible to everyone in the community.

Our Plan

We want to give riders a way to share feedback and ideas to improve bus service. By using the MTA app and this website, we can show which routes need help and suggest ways to make buses faster, more reliable, and fair for everyone. Our goal is to use riders’ voices to make public transit better for the whole community.
